Food in Maharashtra




Puran Poli

Puran Poli is one of Maharashtra’s most loved traditional sweets, especially prepared during festivals like Gudi Padwa, Holi, and Diwali. It is a soft, sweet, and rich flatbread stuffed with a filling of chana dal (Bengal gram) and jaggery, flavoured with cardamom and a hint of nutmeg. The outer layer is made using wheat flour or maida, rolled thin and roasted on a tawa with ghee until golden.



Zunka-Bhakari

Zunka Bhakri is a classic and beloved rural Maharashtrian dish, known for its simplicity, nutrition, and bold flavours. Zunka is a dry, spicy preparation made from besan (gram flour) tempered with mustard seeds, garlic, onions, green chilies, and sometimes curry leaves. It is cooked with minimal water until crumbly and aromatic, making it a perfect match for Bhakri, a rustic flatbread usually made from jowar (sorghum) or bajra (pearl millet).





Chakali

Chakli is a crispy, spiral-shaped savoury snack that holds a special place in every Maharashtrian kitchen, especially during Diwali and other festive occasions. Made with a mix of flours like rice flour, gram flour, and sometimes urad dal flour, Chakli is seasoned with sesame seeds, ajwain (carom seeds), red chili powder, turmeric, and asafoetida.





Vada Pav

Vada Pav is more than just a snack — it's an emotion for every Mumbaikar and Maharashtrian. Often called the "Indian Burger", Vada Pav is a spicy potato fritter (batata vada) sandwiched inside a soft pav (bun), served with a variety of chutneys and a fried green chili on the side.
